About

I am a postdoctoral researcher in laboratory for brain simulation and exploration (SIMEXP) at Montreal Geriatrics Institute (CRIUGM), working with Prof Pierre Bellec. I completed my PhD at the University of York, UK, with Prof Jonny Smallwood.

My current research focuses on brain connectivity-based biomarker discovery through machine learning. I am passionate about data infrastructure and software engineering in scientific research. My open source contribution includes, but not limited to: neuroscience and machine learning software Nilearn, cca-zoo, and pydra workflow task pydra-fsl. You can find out more about my research and open source contribution on my GitHub Profile.

You can also find me at brainhack events. I have recently hosted the OHBM Hackathon 2022 in Glasgow, UK as the OHBM Open Science Special Interest Group Hackathon cochair.

Find my full academic CV here.

When I am not doing research, I spend a lot of time in the kitchen. I am a recreational Olympic weightlifter affiliated with club Les Géants de Montréal. I like video games and TRPG. Not doing them as much as before but still loves chatting about these topics.
